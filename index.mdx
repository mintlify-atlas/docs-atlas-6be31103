MITM-DomainFronting is an open-source technique that lets you access blocked internet services — particularly Google services — without any external VPN server. It works by intercepting your browser's TLS traffic locally (MITM), then re-forwarding it with a spoofed SNI using Domain Fronting through [Xray-core](https://github.com/XTLS/Xray-core).

<Warning>
  This method does not restore full internet access. It currently enables access to specific Google services (Meet, Drive, etc.) from networks where they are restricted. Not all Google services are supported — some (like YouTube videos) have their own infrastructure and some (like Gemini) block Iranian IPs.
</Warning>

## How it works at a glance

MITM-DomainFronting intercepts your browser's HTTPS traffic locally, decrypts it using a trusted self-signed certificate you install, then re-encrypts and forwards it to Google's servers using a fake SNI — bypassing censorship filters that rely on SNI inspection.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Generate a personal certificate">
    Create a self-signed CA certificate using the included `certificate_generator.bat` script or via an online tool. This certificate allows Xray to act as a local MITM proxy.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Install the certificate as trusted">
    Import your certificate into your OS or browser as a Trusted Root CA so your browser accepts the locally-intercepted TLS connections.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Load the Xray configuration">
    Import `MITM-DomainFronting.json` into v2rayN (Windows) or v2rayNG (Android) and activate it as the system proxy.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browse freely">
    Open your browser and access Google Meet, Drive, and other supported services directly — no external server required.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Security notice

<Warning>
  Always generate your own personal certificate. Never use a certificate provided by someone else, and never share your private key (`mycert.key`) with anyone. See the [Security Warnings](/reference/security-warnings) page for full details.
</Warning>
