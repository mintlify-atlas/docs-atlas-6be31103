This page lists what you can and cannot access using MITM-DomainFronting, so you know what to expect before and after setup. The method works by intercepting and re-routing specific traffic — not all traffic — which means coverage is intentionally limited to certain services.

<Info>
  This method does not provide full internet access. It specifically enables certain Google services for users on restricted networks.
</Info>

## Supported services

The following services are accessible through MITM-DomainFronting. All traffic matching `geosite:google` is routed through the H2+H1.1 pipeline, while `googlevideo.com` uses the H1.1 pipeline.

| Service | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Google Meet | Supported | Use via browser on Android (non-rooted) |
| Google Drive | Supported | Use via browser on Android (non-rooted) |
| Google Docs, Sheets, Slides | Supported | Part of `geosite:google` |
| Google Search | Supported | Part of `geosite:google` |
| Gmail | Supported | Part of `geosite:google` |
| Google Calendar | Supported | Part of `geosite:google` |
| General Google services | Supported | All domains in `geosite:google` |
| Google Video CDN / `googlevideo.com` | Supported | Routed via HTTP/1.1 pipeline |

## Not supported or limited

Some services are unavailable due to infrastructure differences or IP-level restrictions that Domain Fronting cannot bypass.

- **YouTube videos** — Not supported. YouTube video delivery uses a separate CDN infrastructure that this method does not cover. The `googlevideo.com` routing handles partial CDN access, but full YouTube video streaming is not guaranteed.
- **Google Gemini** — Not accessible. Gemini blocks Iranian IP addresses at the IP level, not just via SNI. Domain Fronting bypasses SNI-based filtering only; it cannot bypass IP-level blocks.
- **Standalone Android apps (Google Meet app, Google Drive app)** — Cannot use this method on non-rooted Android. Standalone apps do not route through the proxy tunnel for TLS inspection. Use Google Meet or Google Drive in your browser instead.
- **Any service that blocks Iranian IPs directly** — Out of scope for this technique. Domain Fronting addresses SNI-based censorship, not IP-based bans.

## Platform and browser support

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Windows">
    Works system-wide or per-browser depending on where you install the certificate. Install to **Local Machine** → **Trusted Root Certification Authorities** for system-wide coverage, or import into a specific browser's certificate store.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Android (no root)">
    Works in Chrome and Chromium-based browsers. Firefox requires an extra step: go to **Settings** → **About Firefox** → tap the Firefox logo five times → **Settings** → **Secret Settings** → enable **Use third party CA certificates**.

    Standalone apps do **not** work on non-rooted Android — use your browser to access Google Meet, Google Drive, and other services instead.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Linux and macOS">
    Supported. The configuration is the same as on other platforms — import `MITM-DomainFronting.json` into your v2ray-compatible client and install the certificate into your system's trusted root store or browser.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Note>
  As new services become accessible with this method, the project will be updated. Check the [GitHub repository](https://github.com/patterniha/MITM-DomainFronting) for the latest config files and announcements.
</Note>
